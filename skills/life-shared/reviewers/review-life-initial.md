# Reviewer: Life Vision (Initial)

Review the foundational life vision document for goal quality, personal authenticity, and completeness.

**Purpose:** Verify the life vision has real SMART goals with action plans, while still feeling personal.
**Dispatch after:** Life vision document is written.

## What to Check

| Category | What to Look For |
|----------|------------------|
| Goal Specificity | Are goals SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)? Flag vague goals like "be healthier" without specifics. |
| Action Plans | Does each goal have a "how to get there" section? Flag goals that are just wishes without a path. |
| Time Horizons | Are long-term (1-3yr), medium-term (6mo-1yr), and short-term (<6mo) covered for at least some areas? |
| Balance | Are at least 2 life areas explored? Flag if only 1 area was discussed. |
| Authenticity | Does this feel personal or like a generic template? Flag cookie-cutter goals with no personal context. |
| Realism | Are goals achievable? Flag both sandbagging and fantasy targets. |
| Themes | Are cross-area patterns identified? |
| Tone | Should feel personal and warm, not corporate. Flag OKR language, KPIs, or business jargon. |

## Calibration

This is a personal document, not a business plan. Goals should be SMART but written like a human, not a spreadsheet. "Run a 5K by September" is perfect. "Achieve cardiovascular optimization metric Q3" is not.

**Approve if:**
- At least 2 life areas with goals across at least 2 time horizons
- Goals are specific enough to know if they're achieved
- Most goals have some sense of how to get there
- Tone is personal, not corporate

**Revise if:**
- Goals are all vague ("be better at X") with no specifics
- No action plans for any goals
- Only 1 life area explored
- Reads like a corporate strategy document
- Goals are clearly unrealistic for the stated timeframe
