# Life Strategy Templates

Templates for life strategy files. Personal tone, but structured with real goals and action plans.

---

## Life Vision Template

```markdown
---
type: life-vision
created: YYYY-MM-DD
last_reviewed: YYYY-MM-DD
---

# Life Vision

## [Life Area -- e.g., Health & Fitness]

### Long-term (1-3 years)
What do I want my life to look like in this area? What goals am I working toward?

**Goal:** [Specific, measurable goal with timeline]
**How to get there:** [Key steps or approach]

### Medium-term (6 months - 1 year)
What would real progress look like?

**Goal:** [Specific, measurable goal with timeline]
**How to get there:** [Key steps or approach]

### Short-term (less than 6 months)
What can I start working on now?

**Goal:** [Specific, measurable goal with timeline]
**How to get there:** [Key steps or approach]

## [Life Area -- e.g., Relationships & Social]
[Same structure as above]

## [Life Area -- e.g., Personal Growth]
[Same structure as above]

## [Life Area -- e.g., Finance & Lifestyle]
[Same structure as above]

## Themes
Patterns that show up across areas. What connects these goals? Is there an underlying shift that would unlock progress in multiple areas?
```

### Notes on Life Vision
- **Personal but structured** - Write in your own voice, but goals should be specific enough to know if you hit them.
- **SMART goals, naturally stated** - Each goal should have a what, a measure, and a timeline. But write it like a person, not a spreadsheet. "Run a 5K by September" not "Achieve cardiovascular metric of 5K completion by Q3."
- **The HOW matters** - Every goal should have at least a rough plan. A goal without a path is a wish.
- **Only include areas you care about** - Don't force all four areas. Two is fine.
- **Themes tie it together** - Look for patterns. "I need more structure" might apply to health, finances, AND personal growth.

## Monthly Check-in Template

```markdown
---
type: monthly-checkin
year: YYYY
month: month-name
created: YYYY-MM-DD
---

# [Month] Check-in

## Wins This Month
[What went well? What are you proud of? Celebrate before planning.]

## Last Month's Goals Review
- [x] [Goal 1 -- completed]
- [ ] [Goal 2 -- what happened, carry forward / revise / drop]
- [x] [Goal 3 -- completed]

## Updated TODOs
[Anything still active from previous months. Revised items, carried items.]

- [ ] [Active TODO]
- [ ] [Active TODO]

## Goals for Next Month
[2-4 specific, realistic goals achievable in 30 days]

1. [Goal -- specific and measurable]
2. [Goal -- specific and measurable]
3. [Goal -- optional]
4. [Goal -- optional]

## Notes
[Anything else on your mind. Adjustments to the bigger vision. Things that shifted.]
```

### Notes on Monthly Check-ins
- **Wins first** - Always celebrate before planning.
- **Review then update** - Go through last month's goals before setting new ones. Clear the decks.
- **2-4 goals max** - Enough to make progress. Not so many that nothing gets done.
- **Specific and realistic** - "Read 2 books" not "read more." "Save $500" not "save money."
- **Carry-forward is a decision** - Don't silently move missed items. Decide: keep, revise, or drop.

## Weekly Check-in Template

```markdown
---
type: weekly-checkin
year: YYYY
month: month-name
week: N
created: YYYY-MM-DD
---

# Week {N}

## Last Week
- [x] [Accomplished]
- [x] [Accomplished]
- [ ] [Didn't happen -- carried / dropped]

## Monthly Goal Check
[Quick pulse: on track, behind, ahead? Flag anything drifting.]

## This Week's Priorities
1. [Specific action -- maps to monthly goal]
2. [Specific action]
3. [Optional]

## Notes
[Blockers, schedule conflicts, anything relevant]
```

### Notes on Weekly Check-ins
- **Accomplishments first** - What got done before what didn't.
- **Monthly mapping** - Every weekly check should connect to monthly goals.
- **2-4 priorities** - Specific actions, not vague intentions.
- **Flag drift** - If a monthly goal is off-track, name it.
